GSAP Zoetrope

A Zoetrope is a rudimentary animation device invented in the 1800's. The frames of animation are printed on the interior of a rotating cylinder. The cylinder itself has slits cut out so that a viewer may observe the frame directly oposite the slit. When rotated at high speed the slits appear to not move while frames pass in and out of view in a continuous loop.
